## Repository overview

This repository groups five Southeast European language channels into one structured access layer:

- Croatian
- Serbian
- Bosnian
- Albanian
- Romanian

Its purpose is clear: give this regionally coherent cluster a focused and practical place for discovery, integration, and multilingual Islamic content workflows.

---

## Why this repository exists

The Balkans and Southeast Europe carry a historically deep Islamic presence.

Bosnia, Albania, and parts of Serbia and North Macedonia have significant Muslim communities with centuries of Islamic heritage. Croatia and Romania sit within the same regional content-delivery logic.

This repository creates a cleaner layer for:

- regional discoverability
- structured Islamic content access
- multilingual publishing workflows
- localization and deployment
- educational product building
- cleaner repository segmentation

---

## Why this cluster works

Croatian, Serbian, and Bosnian form a highly related South Slavic core — close enough that content often overlaps — while Albanian and Romanian extend the repository into adjacent Southeast European language environments without breaking the regional logic.

This is a geographically honest and linguistically practical cluster.
